Tuberculosis (TB)

Tuberculosis (TB) is an infectious disease caused by the Mycobacterium tuberculosis bacteria. It primarily affects the lungs but can also spread to other parts of the body, such as the brain, spine, and kidneys. TB spreads through the air when an infected person coughs or sneezes.

Symptoms of TB

  • Persistent cough (lasting more than 3 weeks)
  • Coughing up blood or mucus
  • Chest pain
  • Unexplained weight loss
  • Fever and night sweats
  • Fatigue and weakness

Pneumonia

Pneumonia is a lung infection that causes inflammation in the air sacs (alveoli), filling them with fluid or pus. It can be caused by bacteria, viruses, or fungi. Pneumonia can range from mild to life-threatening, especially in young children, the elderly, and people with weakened immune systems.

Symptoms of Pneumonia

  • Cough (with phlegm or dry)
  • Fever, chills, and sweating
  • Difficulty breathing
  • Chest pain when breathing
  • Fatigue and muscle aches
  • Nausea or vomiting, Diarrhea
  • Unexplained weight loss
Test for Pneumonia

COVID-19

COVID-19 (Coronavirus Disease 2019) is an infectious disease caused by the SARS-CoV-2 virus. It was first identified in Wuhan, China, in December 2019 and led to a global pandemic. The disease spreads mainly through respiratory droplets from infected individuals.

Symptoms of COVID-19

  • Fever or chills, Cough ,Shortness of breath
  • Fatigue, Loss of taste or smell, Sore throat
  • Muscle or body aches, Headache, Runny nose or congestion
  • Nausea or vomiting, Diarrhea
  • Fatigue and muscle aches
Test for COVID-19
